What She Kept Seeing

The gap is never a lack of ideas.
It is a lack of architecture.

01
The brief arrives without the real business question in it
There is usually a business pressure underneath a brief — a growth gap, a category tension, a decision still waiting to be made. Most briefs arrive without that pressure named clearly. The thinking that follows is only as strong as what was asked.
02
Good ideas lose force before they reach market
A strong idea can start with real creative depth. Once it moves through feedback, channels, timelines, and approvals — it can lose the thing that made it worth doing. Without a system to hold the idea across execution, ambition becomes a presentation.
03
Spend moves before consumer behaviour has moved
Budget releases on dates, not on proof that consumers have changed. When the campaign ends, neither the brand nor the marketer can answer the one question that matters: did it actually work?
